The lead is one calm, dark prototype. Getting there meant a lot of trying, changing, and cutting. This is that work, kept honest: the research, the concepts, the features built, the interaction models, and the visual directions, including the ones that did not survive.

How it converged

The short version of a long road. Each step narrowed the last.

01
Research the real day
A team lead lives at two altitudes that fight for the same hours. The dread is a deal dying in silence. The scarce resource is trust, already spent by tools that were confidently wrong. That set the brief: less, ranked, and honest about its own confidence.
02
Generate widely, then cut
Thirty concepts across two waves, pushed on three axes: lower the load, make the intelligence visible, go deeper agentic. Most were cut. The survivors are below.
03
Build the ideas to feel them
Twenty-two ideas became standalone, interactive demos, because a triage product is timing and state, not a still picture. You only learn if a microinteraction earns its place by building it.
04
Try the whole screen three ways
The same morning, rebuilt as Quiet Capital, Daybook, and Headline, then fused into twenty variants crossing a visual lineage with a feature and a motion idea. Different points of view, not reskins.
05
The honest detour: warm light
A reviewer called dark "AI slop," so I went warm and editorial to earn calm by daylight. It was honest, but it gave up the atmosphere the product wanted.
06
The answer: a disciplined dark
Atmosphere earned back on purpose. A chosen periwinkle on near-black, warm sand reserved for what needs a human, the same anti-slop rules. The lead prototype is the distillation of everything here.

Everything here is the process behind one answer: a calm, dark morning command center that ranks a team lead's own deals and the team's together, shows its reasoning, and reviews before it sends. The explorations are kept in their original styles on purpose, because they are the journey, not the destination.
