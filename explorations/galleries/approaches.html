The approaches

Three calms.I had to pick one.

Same brief, same user: Dana Reyes, a Team Lead Agent reading her phone before coffee. I designed the morning three different ways - three honest answers to "what does calm look like here?" They are not throwaway sketches. Each is a full, navigable app. The choice between them was the design.

Quiet Capital

The morning as a private bank statement. Monochrome, restrained, the AI almost whispers in serif.

The verdict

I chose Quiet Capital as the base, then grafted on the best of the other two.

Kept from Quiet Capital

Restraint and trust legibility

The whispering base that stays calm under load and scales from 3 items to 30. The spine of the product.

Grafted from Daybook

Color as confidence

Leo's state shows as a leading dot and tag - seaglass, amber, coral - so confidence is readable at a glance.

Grafted from Headline

One-sentence focus

The single most urgent item gets one editorial sentence up top, so the worst leak is unmissable.

Quiet Capital won on the criteria that are non-negotiable for this user: it stays calm when the morning is loud, it scales as Dana's team grows, and it is the most defensible thing to actually ship. But Daybook proved that color is the fastest way to read an AI's confidence, and Headline proved that one giant sentence is the fastest way to surface the worst thing. The final command center is Quiet Capital's restraint carrying Daybook's confidence color and Headline's one-sentence focus - the parts each direction did best, in one feed.
