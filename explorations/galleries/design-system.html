Design system reference
A calm, trust-first AI triage app - voiced by Leo

Design trust, not dashboards.

The visual and behavioral system behind the Leo morning command center: the first screen a real-estate Team Lead opens each day, surfacing only the two or three decisions that need a human. Every choice here serves one job: to make an AI feel calm, legible, and worth trusting. Color carries the AI's confidence. Type carries who is speaking. Motion never hides content.

02

Color

Color encodes the AI's confidence state, never decoration. There is exactly one meaning-bearing color channel here: the AI-state palette. Seaglass is confident. Amber is uncertain. Coral is urgent or wrong. Cobalt is thinking. Ghost-slate is silent. Confidence is always shown twice (color plus type-weight or position) so it survives a glance and color-blindness. Every neutral is tinted toward the canvas hue. We never use pure black or pure white.

The rule. Use OKLCH. Reduce chroma near the lightness extremes. Tint every neutral toward the skin's hue (here, blue at 262). If a color is not carrying AI-state meaning, it is a neutral. No color is ever applied for prettiness alone.

03

Type: the three voices

A human-to-AI product has three speakers, so we type them differently. The split is the signature of the whole system. You should be able to tell who is talking with the screen turned upside down. Serif is only ever the human and Leo. Mono is only ever the machine. Sans is the neutral interface between them.

Voice 01 - human

The voice you trust

Newsreader, serif

Serif is reserved for the human and for Leo's own sentences: the morning greeting, the narration, the calm states, the apology. A serif reads as a person speaking, not a system emitting. It is the warmth in the room.

Display - greeting
Good morning, Dana.
Body - Leo's narration
Overnight I cleared 47 tasks. Three things need you before your 9:30 listing.
Italic - rationale aside
I would counter at $612k. The comps support it.
Voice 02 - interface

The structure

Space, radii, elevation

The five AI states

An AI is never just on or off. It is thinking, sure, unsure, occasionally wrong, or deliberately quiet. Each state has a color, a feeling, and one honest microinteraction. These are live, CSS-only mini-examples. Hover is not required: motion runs on the document timeline so it always renders, even under a strict content-security policy.

Thinking
"Leo is working, and I can see what on."
Negotiation Prep agent
Re-checking 14 comps for 142 Oak St
Feels like: a colleague at the whiteboard. Microinteraction: named skeleton shimmer, not a spinner. The work has a name and a target.
Confident
"Sure, and quiet about it."
Counter sent at $612k. I will alert you the moment they reply.
Negotiation Prep - 14 comps
Feels like: handled, no fanfare. Microinteraction: calm 700ms rise into place plus a source chip. No celebration, no confetti.
Uncertain
"I have a guess, not an answer."
Likely worth between $598k - $624k. The buyer's agent hinted at a deadline I cannot verify.
Want me to confirm before sending?
Feels like: an honest hedge. Microinteraction: lighter type weight, a range instead of a point, and explicit verify copy. Amber asks.
Wrong
"I got one wrong. Here is the fix."
1Acknowledge. I sent the Alvarez follow-up before you approved it.
2Correct. Paused the other 4 in that batch. Undo is open for 14s.
3Escalate. From now I route anything client-facing to you first.
Feels like: a teammate owning a mistake. Microinteraction: acknowledge, correct, escalate. A real apology with an undo window, not an error toast.
Silent

Feels like: a quiet ledger of trust. Microinteraction: the Silence Ledger line. Silence is a designed result, not an empty state.

07

Calibrated confidence

Confidence is categorical, never false precision. A number like "99.73% sure" is a lie dressed as math: it implies a measurement that does not exist and erodes trust the first time it is wrong. Leo speaks in honest bands a human can reason about, and shows the band twice (a word and a color).

Never
99.73% confident

Fake precision. There is no model that knows it is 99.73 rather than 99.74 sure, and the decimal invites a trust it has not earned. Pure performance, zero meaning.

Always
HighAct on it. Leo would proceed.
MediumYour call. Leo has a recommendation.
RangeA band, like $598k to $624k, not a point.

Words and ranges a human can weigh. The color repeats the word, so the band survives a glance and color-blindness.

09

Motion grammar

Motion enhances; it never reveals. Content is visible by default, and every animation is CSS-driven on the document timeline, never a requestAnimationFrame loop. The deliberate engineering note: under a strict content-security policy or a throttled background tab, a JS animation engine can stall and the content vanishes with it. CSS always renders. So entrance, reveal, and state motion never depend on JS, and the whole system degrades gracefully to a fully readable screen.

Easing
cubic-bezier(.16, 1, .3, 1)
One ease-out curve everywhere. Things arrive quickly, then settle, the way a calm hand sets something down.
Durations
100-300ms UI - 600-900ms entrance
UI feedback is near-instant. Entrances breathe over 600 to 900ms. Cinematic moments (the dawn sweep) run 2 to 4s.
Skeletons over spinners
named shimmer, with a target
A loading state shows the shape of what is coming and names the skill doing the work, so waiting feels like progress, not a void.
Content visible by default
no reveal-on-scroll dependency
Nothing is hidden behind a JS reveal. Staggered entrance uses animation-delay with fill-mode both, so content always ends visible.
Reduced motion, authored first
prefers-reduced-motion: reduce
The reduced-motion path was written before the animated one. Every animation collapses to instant; the orb stops breathing; nothing is lost.
All motion is CSS, not JS
graceful degradation by design
Transform and opacity only, will-change only where it animates. JS may layer flourishes for a live demo, but never as the thing that makes content appear.
10

Anti-slop checklist

The rules this system refuses to break. Each one is a common AI-design tell we deliberately reject. This page was self-reviewed against every line.

No side-stripe borders. A colored left edge on a card is an AI tell. State is a leading dot, a tag, or a background tint.
No gradient text. Text is one ink color. Gradients live in the orb and surfaces, never in type.
No pure black or white. Every neutral is tinted toward the canvas hue, in OKLCH, with chroma reduced near the extremes.
No fake precision. Confidence is categorical (High, Medium, ranges), never an invented percentage.
No celebration on confident. Being right is the baseline. No confetti, no badges, no fanfare for doing the job.
No em-dashes or en-dashes. Hyphen only, throughout. A period, comma, colon, or parentheses does the rest.
No motion that hides content. No reveal-on-scroll that can strand a reader behind a stalled JS engine.
No three-voice violations. Serif only for human and Leo, mono only for machine tokens. The split is the signature.
No decorative status dots. A colored dot always means AI state. It is never used as a bullet or ornament.
No filler content. Real names, real-feeling deals. No "Acme", no "John Doe", no invented fake-precise metrics.
